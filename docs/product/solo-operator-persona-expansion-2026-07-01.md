# Memoire Solo Operator Persona Expansion

Date: 2026-07-01

## Decision

Memoire can expand from individual B2B sales professionals to solo operators, as long as the expansion stays anchored in personal sales memory, stuck-deal prevention, and pipeline review readiness.

The recommended umbrella persona is:

> People who sell without a sales team.

Use "solo revenue operator" or "founder-led seller" in internal planning. Use plain-language external copy such as "founders, consultants, freelancers, agency owners, and creators who sell client work, partnerships, or services."

Avoid using "C2B" or "C2C" as the public persona label. Those terms describe market structure, not the acute product pain. The pain Memoire solves is that one person owns the sales memory, buyer context, objections, next actions, and follow-up risk.

## Alignment Assessment

Aligned:

- Founder-led B2B sellers who run their own pipeline.
- Consultants, freelancers, and agency owners selling retainers, projects, implementation work, or advisory engagements.
- Creators and experts selling sponsorships, partnerships, collaborations, workshops, or client services.
- Solo operators using CRM, Excel, Notion, private notes, email threads, or memory to manage long-cycle opportunities.
- Sellers who need to remember account context, avoid missed follow-ups, track objections, and prepare a defensible deal story.

Not aligned:

- Inventory, invoicing, ecommerce, order-management, or marketplace operations.
- Low-consideration transactional C2C selling where there is no meaningful follow-up loop.
- General task management, project management, or personal CRM for all relationships.
- Team CRM, manager forecasting, sales surveillance, or system-of-record workflows.

## Positioning Update

The core wedge remains:

> Catch deals before they go silent.

The broadened positioning is:

> Memoire helps people who sell without a sales team turn scattered customer context into stuck-deal alerts, account memory, next actions, and review-ready deal stories.

Supporting contrast:

> Your CRM or spreadsheet tracks the record. Memoire remembers the story and what needs follow-up.

This keeps the current B2B sales wedge intact while making room for solo business sellers who may not identify as traditional sales reps.

## Product Copy Direction

Use:

- "people who sell without a sales team"
- "founder-led sellers"
- "solo operators"
- "consultants, freelancers, agency owners, and creators selling client work"
- "client, buyer, partner, or account context"
- "review-ready deal story"
- "stuck-deal alerts"

Use sparingly:

- "individual B2B sales professionals"
- "manager-ready" when the workflow is specifically weekly pipeline review
- "CRM" only as a contrast, not as the required source of truth

Avoid:

- "business management app"
- "client operating system"
- "personal CRM for everything"
- "C2B/C2C app"
- "invoice, project, inventory, ecommerce, or marketplace management"

## Landing Page Changes

Recommended public surface:

- Keep the headline or wedge around stuck deals and pipeline review.
- Broaden the eyebrow and supporting copy from "B2B sellers" to "B2B and solo operators."
- Add audience bullets for founder-led sales, consultants, agencies, freelancers, and creators with partnership/client pipelines.
- Keep the "Not a CRM" section, but mention spreadsheets and private notes alongside CRM.
- Keep "not ideal" copy explicit: no ecommerce, inventory, invoicing, team CRM, or marketplace management.

## Onboarding Changes

The onboarding should let a solo operator understand the workflow without needing a manager:

- Capture messy customer/client evidence.
- Open Today to see what can go silent.
- Review account/client memory.
- Draft or add the missing follow-up.
- Prepare a review-ready deal story for yourself, a partner, a client, or a manager when relevant.

Do not add new onboarding paths for invoicing, delivery, project execution, or general business tasks.

## Demo Data Direction

The current demo can remain technical B2B because it proves the strongest wedge. Add a later solo-operator demo pack only after the current demo QA remains stable.

Suggested future sample accounts:

- "Northstar Advisory" selling a strategy retainer with a missing budget owner.
- "Studio Lane" selling a brand project with an unresolved scope objection.
- "Creator Partnerships" selling a sponsor package with no confirmed decision date.
- "Agency implementation" selling a setup project with unclear procurement or stakeholder ownership.

Each sample should still map to account memory, opportunity, objection, next action, and stuck-deal risk. No invoice, inventory, ecommerce, or delivery-management objects should be introduced.

## Pricing And Packaging

The current Solo / Pro / Team-later hypothesis still works if Solo is defined as:

> For one person managing their own sales follow-up and pipeline memory.

Do not create separate packages for "freelancer," "agency," or "creator" yet. Segment fit should be tested through early-access intake and cohort interviews before changing billing.

## 2026-07-02 Founder Decision: Individual-Client Long-Cycle Selling Included

The founder confirmed the target includes solo entrepreneurs selling to individual clients (C2B and long-cycle C2C), not only business buyers. The alignment test is behavioral, not the market label: the deal must have a follow-up loop long enough for silence to be a real risk.

- Included: solo entrepreneurs and solo businesses selling high-value, long-cycle work to businesses or individual clients (freelance projects, sponsorships, advisory, personal brokerage/high-ticket services).
- Still excluded: quick transactional selling with no meaningful follow-up loop (same-day C2C, marketplace order flow) — this remains outside the wedge and the public "not ideal" copy now states it.
- Public copy still avoids the literal "C2B/C2C" labels and describes the behavior instead.

Instrumentation shipped 2026-07-02:

- Request Access role options now include "Solo entrepreneur / Solo operator".
- Request Access segment options now include "High-value sales to individual clients".
- Landing audience bullets include solo entrepreneurs selling to businesses or individual clients; "not ideal" list names quick transactional selling.

Cohort Wave 1 should report how many intake requests select these options and whether that segment activates without the manager-review framing before any packaging or demo-pack changes are made for it.
