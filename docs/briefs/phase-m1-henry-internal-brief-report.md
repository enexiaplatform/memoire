## 2. Strategic Direction

Memoire is being tested as a Pipeline Review Defense Brief for technical B2B sales managers and player-managers.

The working wedge is:

> Help the sales owner defend, rescue, downgrade, or clarify weak deals before pipeline review.

This direction shifts Memoire away from a broad Sales Memory App and toward a weekly manager-use artifact that exposes weak forecast evidence, missing decision context, unresolved objections, and deals at risk of going silent.

## 3. Why Henry Is The First Tester

Henry is the founder and a real sales manager / player-manager in technical B2B life science sales.

His own pipeline review pain is directly relevant because he needs to:

- defend forecasted opportunities,
- identify which deals are based on evidence versus hope,
- catch missing decision context,
- resolve technical and procurement objections,
- decide which deals to rescue, downgrade, or monitor,
- prepare clear answers before review.

Henry's prior critiques and reflections count as internal user feedback because they come from the exact operating environment Memoire is now trying to serve.

## 4. What This Brief Is Meant To Validate

The Phase M.1 brief validates whether a weekly Memoire output can change real sales actions.

Specifically, Henry should test whether the brief helps him answer:

- Which deals are weak before pipeline review?
- Which deals are missing decision context?
- Which objections are unresolved?
- Which forecasted deals are based on evidence versus hope?
- What should Henry ask or do this week?
- Which deals should be defended, downgraded, or rescued?

The most important validation question:

> Does the brief make Henry take a better action this week than he would have taken without Memoire?

## 6. Next Step After Henry Reviews The Brief

Henry should use the sample and template against his real weekly pipeline.

Recommended next step:

1. Fill the template for one real review period.
2. Mark every deal as Defensible, Weak but recoverable, Hope-based, or Unsupported.
3. Use the evaluation checklist immediately after the review.
4. Record which actions changed because of the brief.
5. Decide whether Memoire should productize this as the next core weekly output.

Product decision after review:

- If the brief changes real actions, Memoire should prioritize the Pipeline Review Defense Brief workflow.
- If the brief is useful but too manual, identify the minimum data source needed to automate it.
- If the brief feels obvious, Memoire should sharpen evidence extraction and manager-question quality before building more UI.
